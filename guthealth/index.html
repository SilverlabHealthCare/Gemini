From Symptom Management to Immune Fortification

South African consumers are moving beyond simply treating "upset stomachs." There is a growing clinical awareness that 70-80% of the immune system resides in the gut. This has shifted buying patterns from reactive (treating food poisoning after it happens) to proactive (building a "stomach immunity" barrier).

Prescription (Rx) Market Snapshot

Heavy Antiemetics Demand spikes seasonally (Nov-Jan) during "stomach flu" peaks in South Africa. Prescription volumes for Schedule 4 anti-nausea meds are rising as viral strains become more aggressive.
Post-Infection Probiotics Doctors are increasingly prescribing high-colony count probiotics alongside antibiotics to prevent secondary gastric distress, bridging the gap between clinical and self-care.
Bacterial Colitis Food poisoning cases requiring prescription antibiotics (like Ciprofloxacin) account for only ~12% of total gastric events, suggesting the OTC market handles 88% of cases.
